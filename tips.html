Joel's Tips

How I find books worth reading, where I get them, and the small habits that keep me reading more without making it feel like homework.

01

Where I Look for Books

[Placeholder — write about the channels you trust most. Friends? A specific critic or newsletter? A favorite indie bookstore's staff picks shelf?]

  • Recommendations from people I trust — the single best signal.
  • Newsletters and reviews — a few I check regularly.
  • Bookstore browsing — staff picks tables, end caps, the displays at the front.
  • One book leading to another — author interviews, footnotes, "if you liked this" lists.
02

How I Decide What's Worth My Time

[Placeholder — what makes you commit to a book vs. set it aside. The first 50 pages rule? A reviewer you trust? A topic you're chasing?]

  • The opening pages — does the writing pull me in?
  • The premise — am I curious about the question this book is asking?
  • Permission to quit — life is short; not every book is for me right now.
03

How I Actually Get Books

[Placeholder — your mix of library, used, new, ebook, physical, audiobook. What you reach for first and why.]

  • Library + Libby — the default for most things; free and effortless.
  • Used bookstores — for the ones I know I'll keep.
  • Buying new — when I want to support an author or grab something on release.
  • Audiobooks — for walks, drives, and kitchen time.
04

Tracking and Notes

[Placeholder — your system, however light. A notes app? Index cards in the back of the book? Just this site?]

  • This site — short, public, easy to keep up with.
  • A short note when I finish — the one thing I'd want to remember.
  • A "to read" list — kept somewhere I'll actually look at it.
